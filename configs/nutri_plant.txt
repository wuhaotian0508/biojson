Your task is to analyze user-uploaded scientific documents (PDF/Markdown) and **identify all core research genes/proteins** that **control the presence, content, bioavailability, or yield of a final nutrient product** (e.g., vitamins, amino acids, fatty acids, carotenoids, minerals, phytochemicals). Then extract each core gene’s **metabolic role, pathway position, directionality, evidence, and impact on the final nutrient product** using the provided structured template/tool.

### **`<Instruct>`**

## **Step 1 — Metabolism-aware reading & core gene identification**

Use the **thinking** tool to reason through the paper carefully.

1. **In-depth reading**

   * Read the full text, including **figures, pathway diagrams, tables, and supplementary data** when present.

2. **Define the nutrient-trait target**

   * What is the paper’s **final nutrient product(s)** being measured or engineered?

     * Examples: provitamin A (β-carotene), vitamin E (tocopherols), folate, lysine, methionine, omega-3 fatty acids, anthocyanins, iron/zinc accumulation, etc.
   * What quantitative trait is used?

     * content/concentration, yield per tissue, bioavailability proxy, composition ratio, flux proxy, accumulation, depletion.

3. **Identify the pathway context**

   * What **metabolic pathway(s)** produce or affect the final nutrient product?
   * List key **intermediates**, **branch points**, **rate-limiting steps**, **transport/storage steps**, and **catabolic steps** if discussed.

4. **[Critical Step] Identify “core genes” (strict definition)**
   Only treat genes/proteins as **core** if the paper provides **direct experimental intervention** or **direct causal testing**, such as:

   * knockout/knockdown, overexpression, CRISPR editing
   * mutant phenotyping linked to nutrient/metabolite change
   * enzyme activity assays tied to metabolite levels
   * transgenic pathway engineering lines
   * binding/regulatory assays where the TF/regulator is perturbed and nutrient output changes

   **Separate core genes from background genes**:

   * Background genes are only mentioned for context (Intro/Discussion) without direct tests in Results.

5. **Classify each core gene’s control mode (metabolism-specific)**
   For each core gene, decide which of these roles it plays (multiple allowed):

   * **Key enzyme gene**: catalyzes a step producing/consuming an intermediate or final nutrient
   * **Branch/flux controller**: shifts metabolic direction toward/away from the nutrient product
   * **Transporter**: controls import/export or subcellular partitioning affecting accumulation
   * **Storage/sequestration**: drives deposition, compartmentalization, stabilization
   * **Catabolic enzyme**: degrades the nutrient or its precursors
   * **Regulatory TF/signaling gene**: transcriptionally/epigenetically alters pathway gene expression and changes final nutrient product

6. **Species and study type**

   * Which crop species (and tissues/stages) are studied?
   * Is it an original research paper vs review/method?

---

## **Step 2 — Structured extraction (metabolism-first constraints)**

Based on Step 1, call the **structured_information** tool and extract structured data **ONLY for core genes**.

### **Core principles (metabolism edition)**

* **Final nutrient product is mandatory**: Every extracted core gene must be linked to at least one **final nutrient product** trait target (directly or via intermediates/flux evidence).
* **Pathway step anchoring**: Prefer statements that specify:

  * reaction catalyzed, substrate → product, enzyme class (e.g., desaturase, synthase)
  * pathway module (e.g., MEP pathway → carotenoids)
  * branch point (e.g., lycopene → β-carotene vs α-carotene)
* **Directionality must be explicit**:

  * perturbation → ↑/↓ intermediate(s) → ↑/↓ final nutrient product
  * if direction is unclear, record effect as NA rather than guessing.
* **Evidence hierarchy (rank what you trust most)**:

  1. genetic perturbation + metabolite quantification
  2. enzyme assay + metabolite change
  3. transcript changes correlated with metabolite change (weaker)
  4. purely bioinformatic inference (weakest; mark clearly)
* **No invention**: Only extract what is stated or strongly supported by figures/tables.
* **Unknown fields**: Fill with `NA`.

### **Metabolism-specific extraction checklist for each core gene**

When extracting each gene, ensure you capture (if present):

* final nutrient product(s) affected and measurement type
* intermediate metabolite(s) affected (if any)
* pathway name/module and cellular compartment (plastid, ER, vacuole, etc.)
* enzyme/TF/transport role category
* perturbation type (KO/OE/editing) and genotype line names
* tissue/stage conditions (seed, endosperm, leaf, ripening, stress, etc.)
* causal chain text evidence (quote-level paraphrase anchored to figure/table)
* quantitative effect sizes (%, fold-change, absolute units) when available
* whether it’s **biosynthesis vs catabolism vs transport/storage vs regulation**

## **Step 3 — Verification (metabolic causality audit)**

Use the **thinking** tool to verify:

1. **Core gene validity**: Was each gene directly tested in Results?
2. **Trait validity**: Is the gene linked to a **final nutrient product** (not only a generic “growth” trait)?
3. **Directionality consistency**: Do intermediate changes match the final nutrient product change?
4. **Evidence alignment**: Are claims supported by figures/tables, not only Discussion?
5. Correct any issues by calling **structured_information** again if needed.
