# ABSTRACT

Harder kernels of barley are regarded as one of the factors that restrict water and enzyme movement within the endosperm during malting. A comprehensive study of two domestic varieties was performed for evaluating malting quality. Both $\beta$ -glucan and total protein content of the Chinese domestic barley (Ganpi-6 and Kenpi-14) were significantly higher than Copeland. Grain hardness of the Chinese domestic barley was higher and water uptake ratio was lower compared with the Copeland. During germination, the expression levels of NCED1, NCED2 (major key regulatory enzymes for abscisic acid biosynthesis genes) were higher, whereas gibberellic acid (GA) synthesis genes (GA20ox1, GA2ox3, GA3ox2) were lower in the Ganpi-6, Kenpi-14 compared with Copeland. These two domestic barley varieties also showed significantly lower limit dextrinase and $\beta$ -glucanase activity compared with Copeland. Ultrasound treatment improved the malting quality of Ganpi-6 by enhancing water uptake and GA synthesis gene expression increased. Therefore, these findings provided insights into the future direction on the utilization of ultrasonication for the applications towards the improvement of the harder barley variety. 

# 1. Introduction

The process of malting involves barley steeped in water and germinated under proper conditions and kilning. Malts are typically made from cereal grains and are used for production of beverages. The degradation of main proteins into peptides or free amino acids, and nonstarch polysaccharides into $\beta$ -glucan, arabinoxylan occur primarily during malting [1]. Also, the degradation of starch into fermentable sugars (such as maltotriose, maltose and glucose) occur primarily during malting but the majority occurs during mashing [1]. Seed germination initiates with water uptake by the dry seed and is of great importance for malt quality improvement [2]. The physical structure of barley kernels appeared to be vital to the different water rate [3-11]. Hydration is influenced by various factors, including seed morphology (such as micropyle, seed coat permeability, and testa and pericarp structure), endosperm hydration efficiency, and water availability. Landau et al observed that in stirling barley from Australia, the presence of a channel running from the ventral scutellum to the distal end of the grain played a critical role in this relationship [11]. The rate of barley hydration during steeping is influenced by various factors, including water temperature 

[12]. Elevated temperatures exceeding the typical range of $12 - 16^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ can accelerate hydration but may lead to detrimental effects during germination [12,13]. The steeping process for different barley varieties is crucial in determining immersion duration, air rest intervals, and overall moisture content, all of which impact malt quality [9,14]. Barley seed hardness is an important character of cereal quality influencing malting [15,16]. The steely endosperm of barley kernels is tougher, often accompanied by the slower water uptake and insufficient enzyme distribution during steeping and malting [17]. Grain hardness related to the texture of the kernel and mainly determined by a number of factors relating to the endosperm structure and composition [18]. In addition, grain hardness mainly determined by the adhesion degree of the storage protein and starch, rather than the hardness of the individual components of the seeds [19]. On the other hand, the amounts of cell wall polysaccharides (such as $\beta$ -glucan, arabinoxylan), starch (amylose, amylopectin), proteins, and lipid have also been proposed as important factors affect the hardness of the endosperm [17,18]. 

Ultrasound (US) is a form of energy generated by acoustic waves at frequencies higher than $20\mathrm{kHz}$ [20]. US can break seeds dormancy, improve the germination rate, and shorten the germination time [21]. 

Studies showed that US treatments have shown promising results in many plants, including tall fescue, rice, beans, corn, fern spores, peanut, wheat, chickpea, and watermelon [22]. These researches implied that ultrasound treatment can improve seed germination. But the mechanisms by which ultrasound promotes seed germination are still unknown. Previous research suggested that sonication can dislodge adherent microbe from grain surfaces [23]. In comparison to control, the remarkably reduced microbial contaminations led to faster and more homogenous germination [23]. Recent studies have revealed that proper US treatment can increase the enzyme activities or promote cell growth by stimulating the vitality of plant cells [24]. Specifically, the energy produced by the US can enhance the peroxidase and amylase activity, promoting the division of cells and increasing the division speed and growth [22,25]. Besides, macromolecules (eg. carbohydrates, amino acids) can generate some new materials under the treatment of US, and these new compounds can participate in growth regulation and metabolism [25,26]. In addition, appropriate ultrasonic intensity activates the cell membrane produces small pores which facilitates the ions and metabolites transmembrane transport, further triggering the biochemical, physiological changes of cells and controlling gene expression [26]. 

Additionally, although many studies investigated various methods to improve seed germination and plant growth such as plant growth regulators, seed priming and dry heat treatments [27]. The advantages of using US technique are simple, environmentally friendly, energy saving, cost-effective and may have multiple positive effects on plant performance [28]. As US technology has been widely used in the food industry by enhanced the hydration process of seeds and grains [28]. Thus, the purpose of the present study was to address questions concerning water uptake in barley seeds. 

# 2. Materials and methods

# 2.1. Barley samples

The samples of barley were provided by the Tsingtao Brewery Co. Ltd. (Qingdao, China) including varieties Copeland, Ganpi-6 and Kenpi-14. These varieties were both two-row barley that had been separately purchased from China and Canada in 2019. All samples were stored at $4^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ to maintain their viability. 

# 2.2. Grain $\beta$ -glucan assay

The $\beta$ -glucan of barley was determined according to the methods reported in the literature [29]. Grain $\beta$ -glucan was measured with the Megazyme mixed-linkage $\beta$ -glucan assay according to the manufacturer's protocol. There were three replicates for each sample. 

# 2.3. Grain protein assay

Total protein of barley was calculated from the nitrogen content measured by the Kjeldahl method (1883). Each measurement was conducted in triplicate. 

# 2.4. Grain arabinoxylan content assay

Total grain arabinoxylan content was measured by the phloroglucinol method described by previous report [30]. 

# 2.5. Total starch content and amylose content

Total starch content was measured using the total starch assay kit (K-TSTA, Megazyme) according to the manufacturer's protocol. Amylose content was measured using the amylose/amylopectin assay kit of Megazyme (K-AMYL). Each measurement was conducted in triplicate. 

# 2.6. Grain hardness test

The hardness of barley was determined by the texture analysis according to the methods reported in the literature [31]. It was performed with FTC texture analyzer (Food Technology Corporation, US). A three-inch compression plate was installed in the $25\mathrm{-kg}$ load cell of the analyzer. The sample was tested in speed of $60~\mathrm{mm / min}$ with $1.5\mathrm{N}$ trigger force. All samples were compressed and the obtained texture profiles were used to measure the hardness. The maximum positive force (N) is the force required to penetrate the barley to the specified distance. The maximum positive force during the first compression cycle was measured and referred to as hardness. All treatments were measured with 25 replications. 

# 2.7. Water uptake assay

Approximately $0.2\mathrm{g}$ grains (detailed data were recorded) from each cultivar were weighed. Then the sample was placed in a porous basket and soaked in a water bath at $22^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ . The basket was removed from the water bath at different time points as indicated in figure legends. Filter paper was used to remove the residual water at the grain surface and the samples were reweighed. The mass increase during soaking was used for the water uptake ratio calculation [32]. There were three replicates for each data point. 

# 2.8. Germination experiment

100 barley seeds were imbibed with $4\mathrm{mL}$ deionized water in a $9\mathrm{-cm}$ Petri dish. The Petri dishes were incubated in the dark at $18^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for 5 days. Samples were taken on the first, third, fifth day and subjected to further analyses as indicated. The experiment was conducted three replications. 

# 2.9. RNA extraction and quantitative real-time PCR (qRT-PCR)

Green malt samples collected from the germination experiment were assessed for gene expression. Collected samples were immediately frozen in liquid nitrogen and then kept in $-80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for RNA extraction. Then samples were ground and TRIzol (Invitrogen, USA) was added, total RNA was extracted according to the manufacturer's instructions of GeneJET plant RNA purification mini kit (Thermo Scientific) with minor modification. TURBO DNA-free Kit (Thermo Scientific) was used to digest genomic DNA. RNA reverse transcription was performed with PrimeScript™ RT master mix (Takara) according to the manufacturer's instructions. qRT-PCR was performed using the TB Green® premix Ex Taq™ (Takara) following the manufacturer's instructions. Actin was used as an internal control and the primers were listed in Table S1. 

# 2.10. Limit dextrinase and amylase activity assays

Limit dextrinase (LD) and amylase activity were assayed according to the protocols of Megazyme Limit DextriZyme, Cerelpha and Betamyl-3 assay kit (TLDZ200, KCERA, K-BETA) with some modification [33]. Each measurement was conducted in triplicate. 

# 2.11. $\beta$ -glucanase activity assays

$\beta$ -glucanase activity was measured by Megazyme $\beta$ -glucanase assay (K-MBGL) according to the manufacturer's protocol. Each measurement was conducted in triplicate. 

# 2.12. Free amino nitrogen content and sugars composition

Malt samples were mashed to make wort using a simple method [33]. FAN content was measured with a continuous flow analyzer (Skalar, the Netherlands). Sugars composition analysis were conducted with HPLC 

(Waters Alliance 2695, USA), appropriate concentrations of glucose, sucrose, maltose, fructose, maltotetraose, and maltotriose (Sigma-Aldrich) were used as internal standards. 

# 2.13. Micro-malting process

The micro-malting was conducted with an artificial climate box (LTH-275 N, LongYue, China) during steeping and germination period in this study. $400\mathrm{g}$ barley grains were steeped for $36\mathrm{h}$ (steeping in water for $8\mathrm{h}$ , and then resting in air for $8\mathrm{h}$ , steeping in water for $8\mathrm{h}$ , and resting in air for $8\mathrm{h}$ , steeping in water for $4\mathrm{h}$ ) and germinated for $96\mathrm{h}$ at $16^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , $95\%$ relative humidity. The kilning of green malts was carried out for $10\mathrm{h}$ at $55^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , then $4\mathrm{h}$ at $65^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , followed with $80^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for $5\mathrm{h}$ . The rootlets and protruding acrospires of kilned malt were removed by hand. Before steeping, barley seeds were soaked in the ultrasound machine under turned on mode for $40\mathrm{min}$ referred as ultrasound treatment, while under same condition with turned off mode was considered as control. The micro-malting had three replicates for each sample. 

# 2.14. Wort preparation

Each malt $(50\mathrm{g})$ was milled with laboratory disc mill and mixed with $200~\mathrm{mL}~45^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ deionized water in a stainless-steel vessel. Then the mash temperature was held at $45\pm 0.1^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for $30\mathrm{min}$ accompanied by stirring. Then the temperature was raised to $70^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ , $100~\mathrm{mL}$ deionized water $(70^{\circ}\mathrm{C})$ was added, followed held at $70\pm 0.1^{\circ}\mathrm{C}$ for $1\mathrm{hr}$ accompanied by stirring. Then the temperature was cooling to room temperature and proper amount deionized water was added to make the wort to $450~\mathrm{g}$ . The wort was filtered and then used for quality analysis. 

# 2.15. Moisture content, wort chromaticity, wort viscosity, wort turbidity assays

Malt and wort quality, including moisture content, wort chromaticity, wort viscosity, wort turbidity, were measured using the EBC Analytica methods. 

# 2.16. SOD, CAT and POD activity analysis

The antioxidant enzymes such as superoxide dismutase (SOD), catalase (CAT) and peroxidase (POD) activity were determined according to the protocols of a commercialized chemical assay kit (Grace Biotechnology, China). 

# 2.17. Statistical analysis

All experiments were carried out in triplicate. Means and standard deviation of data obtained were calculated and the significance was accepted at $\mathrm{P} < 0.05$ .

# 3.2. GA, ABA synthesis genes expression and enzyme activity comparison between the domestic barley Ganpi-6, Kenpi-14 and imported barley Copeland

Phytohormone contents, and their signaling pathway play important roles in seed physiological state determination and germination process regulation [36]. The germinate not germinate switch was basically determined by the ratio of GA to ABA and ABA's catabolism [37]. Endogenous ABA content was decreased during imbibition and early germination phase. Nine-cis-epoxycarotenoid dioxygenaes (NCEDs) was the major key regulatory enzymes for ABA biosynthesis [38]. The germination-inhibiting effect of ABA was counteracted by gibberellins (GAs), GA20 and GA3 oxidases (GA20ox and GA3ox) were the major key regulatory enzymes for GA biosynthesis [36-38]. Transcripts of GA20ox 

and GA3ox were accumulated during early germination [39]. The transcriptional changes of key GA and ABA biosynthesis enzymes were analyzed during the early phase of germination (8 h) of domestic barley and Copeland seeds (Fig. 2A). # 3.4. Ultrasound treatment during malting improved the malt quality by water uptake enhanced, antioxidant enzymes decreased and GA synthesis gene expression increased

From the results described above, it can be concluded that the qualities of domestic barley were relatively poor due to its harder kernels compared with imported barley. Several studies suggested ultrasound treatment promote seed germination in a variety of plant species, including wheat, peanut, and rice. Some studies found that several germinate related enzymes activities was increased after ultrasoundation, including $\alpha$ -amylase [47,48]. The cleaning effect on brewing barley using vibrations during wet steeping had been reported before [23]. Sonication can remove adherent microbe from grain surfaces and remarkably reduced microbial contaminations, led to faster and more homogenous germination [23]. To investigated the effects of ultrasound 

on Chinese domestic (Ganpi-6) malting, barley seeds were soaked in the ultrasound washing machine under turned on mode for $40\mathrm{min}$ referred as ultrasound treatment, while under same condition with turned off mode was considered as control. The increased water uptake ratio during steeping at various time points were monitored (Fig. 4A). These results showed that the increased water uptake ratio of Ganpi-6 was much lower than Copeland. However, the increased ratio of Ganpi-6 (Ultrasonic) was remarkably higher than Ganpi-6 (Fig. 4A). After the micro-malting was completed, the following malt characteristics were measured: moisture content, wort chromaticity, wort sugar concentration, wort viscosity, wort turbidity, FAN, $\beta$ -glucan, $\alpha$ -amylase activity, beta-amylase activity, LD, $\beta$ -gluanse, sugar profile (Table 1). As shown in Table 1, the content of wort chromaticity, wort sugar concentration, FAN, $\beta$ -glucan, $\alpha$ -amylase activity, LD activity, $\beta$ -gluanse activity in Ganpi-6 were significantly lower than Copeland. For the analysis of sugar profile, maltose and sucrose, maltotetraose ratio in the Ganpi-6 group were significantly higher than that in the Copeland (Table. 1). Total sugar content, maltotriose and glucose ratio in Ganpi-6 were significantly lower than control (Table. 1). After treatment with ultrasonic, all the indicators improved significantly compared with control (Ganpi-6), such as wort chromaticity, wort viscosity, wort turbidity, FAN, $\beta$ -glucan, $\alpha$ -amylase activity, LD activity, $\beta$ -gluanse activity (Table. 1). This means that ultrasound treatment of the harder barley may really improve the malting performance. But why did moderate ultrasound produce a slight increase in malting performance for domestic barley? 

One of the primary causes of seed deterioration was the excessive accumulation of reactive oxygen species (ROS). In 1935, Thompson and Kosar found that the germination of lettuce seed stimulated by chemical treatment, such as thiourea, allyl thiourea, ammonium thiocyanate and potassium thiocyanate [49]. During seed germination, antioxidant 

compounds concentration such as ascorbate, glutathione, and peroxidases were increased [50,51]. In Lolium rigidum gaud seeds, low dormancy seeds were more prepared for rapid germination via peroxidase-mediated cell wall weakening [52]. Antioxidant enzymes and small molecular antioxidants played a complementary role during soybean seed germination [53]. Previous studies have suggested that ROS can damage proteins, nucleic acids, amino acids, and membrane lipids [54,55]. The antioxidant enzymes SOD, CAT, POD activity was found to be higher in Ganpi-6 compared to Copeland (Fig. 4B). Regarding antioxidant enzymatic activity after ultrasonic treatment, the Ganpi-6(Ultrasonic) group preserved a lower SOD, POD activity when compared to the control (Ganpi-6) group (Fig. 4B). The transcripts of NCED1, NCED2 were lower in the Ganpi-6(Ultrasonic) during early germination (8 h after steeping), the expression levels of $GA20ox1$ , $GA20x3$ and $GA30x2$ were higher in the Ganpi-6(Ultrasonic) group compared with Ganpi-6 at the end of germination (Fig. 4C-D). Therefore, these findings indicated that ultrasound treatment during malting improved the malt quality of Ganpi-6 by water uptake enhanced, antioxidant enzymes decreased and GA/ABA synthesis gene expression increased/decreased.

Harder kernels of barley seed in Ganpi-6 and Kenpi-14 indicated a lower water uptake ratio and GA synthesis gene expression level.
