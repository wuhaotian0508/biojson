# Supplementary Methods

Plant Growth Conditions Sucrose/glucose feeding experiment were performed with seedlings grown on AT medium (pH5.8, $0 . 6 \%$ Agar) supplemented with 30 mM glucose or $1 5 ~ \mathrm { m M }$ sucrose. Glc supplemented plants were treated for 2 hours with TOR inhibitor (AZD8055, $5 ~ \mu \mathrm { M } )$ or antimycin A (AMA, $1 ~ \mu \mathrm { M } )$ , an inhibitor of mitochondrial respiration due to binding to the Qi site of cytochrome c reductase. 

For sulfide fumigation experiment, wild type plants were grown on soil for 5 weeks and then fumigated with 1 ppm $\mathrm { H } _ { 2 } \mathbf { S }$ for 6 hours. 

All plants were grown in a short-day climate chamber (8.5 h light/15.5 h dark; 80-100 μmol m-2s-1; $2 2 ^ { \circ } \mathbf { C }$ day/ $1 8 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ night; $5 0 \%$ humidity). 

Genomic Characterization Genomic DNA from leaf or root material was isolated as outlined in Edwards, Johnstone 10. For genomic characterization of homozygous plants, PCR was performed according to GLP standards, with the isolated gDNA and specific primer pair combinations outlined in Supplementary Table 4. 

Determination of SERAT Enzyme Activity SERAT activity in leaves was determined as described in Heeg et al., 2008, with the only difference that 2 units purified recombinant OAS-TL 11 was used to achieve the abundance of OAS-TL activity. The assay was performed in a total volume of $1 0 0 ~ \mu \mathrm { l }$ containing $5 8 ~ \mu \mu$ protein extract from $1 0 0 ~ \mathrm { { m g } }$ plant material, 50 mM Hepes/KOH pH 7.5, 10 mM Na2S, 5 mM DTT, $1 0 ~ \mathrm { m M }$ serine and 2 unites OASTL. The reaction mix was incubated at $2 5 \mathrm { { ^ \circ C } }$ for 5 min. Then $1 \ \mathrm { m M }$ acetylcoA was added and incubated at $2 5 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ for $3 0 ~ \mathrm { m i n }$ . The reaction was stopped by addition of $5 0 ~ \mu \mathrm { ~ 2 0 \% ~ }$ TCA. After centrifugation, the supernatant was transferred quantitatively to $2 0 0 ~ \mu \mathrm { l }$ ninhydrin and $1 0 0 ~ \mu \mathrm { l }$ acetic acid and boiled at $1 0 0 \mathrm { { } ^ { \circ } C }$ for 10 min. Then samples were cooled down and mixed with $5 5 0 ~ \mu \mathrm { ~ 1 0 0 \% ~ }$ ethonal. The derivatives of ninhydrincysteine was measured at $5 6 0 \mathrm { n m }$ with the photometer UvikonXL. 

Total RNA extraction RNA was extracted from $5 0 ~ \mathrm { m g }$ of plant materials using the peqGOLD total RNA kit (peqLAB, Erlangen). The manufacturer’s protocol was slightly modified to optimize the extraction. Briefly plant material was homogenized in $4 0 0 ~ \mu \mathrm { l }$ lysis buffer T. DNase digestion was performed on the RNA binding column for 15 min at room temperature. RNA was eluted by 50 µl RNase free water. RNA concentration was determined by Nanodrop. 

cDNA synthesis RNA was converted to cDNA using cDNA synthesis kit (Fermentas), according to the manufacturer’s protocol with $0 . 5 ~ \mu \xi$ RNA and $0 . 5 ~ \mu \mathrm { l }$ oligo dT primers (for SULTR1;1, APR2 and SDI1 detection) or 25s RNA_RT primer (Supplementary Table 4, for 25s rRNA detection). RNA was incubated with oligo dT at $6 5 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ for 5 min. Then cDNA synthesis was carried out at $4 2 \mathrm { { ^ \circ C } }$ for 1 hour. The synthesized cDNA for mRNA detection was diluted 1:5 for qPCR. The synthesized cDNA for 25s rRNA detection was diluted 1:10000 for qPCR. 25s rRNA was measured according to Ren et al., 20113. 

qRT-PCR qRT-PCR was performed using the SYBR mix (qPCRBIOSyGreen Mix Lo-ROX) in the RotorQ cycler. Relative expression data was normalized against the expression of the reference gene TIP41-like (primers listed in Supplementary Table 4). 

Edu staining 7-day old seedlings grown on AT medium (pH5.8, $0 . 6 \%$ Agar) supplemented with $3 0 ~ \mathrm { m M }$ Glc were transferred to AT medium supplemented with 30 mM Glc and $5 ~ \mu \mathrm { M }$ AZD8055 (TOR inhibitor) or $5 ~ \mu \mathrm { M }$ AMA (Mitochondria inhibitor) for 90 min. Then $5 ~ { \mu \mathrm { ~ l } } { \mu \mathrm { M } }$ EdU in liquid AT medium was added directly on the root tip. The root tips were incubated with EdU for 30 min in the climate chamber. Then the seedlings were fixed in $1 0 0 ~ \mu \mathrm { l }$ Fixation/Permeabilization reagent $4 \%$ Formaldehyde, $0 . 1 \%$ Triton X-100 in 1x PBS) for 30 min. After fixation, seedlings were incubated in the dark with $1 0 0 ~ \mu \mathrm { l }$ Click-iT reaction cocktail (prepared according to the manufacturer’s protocol, Click-iT $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ EdU Alexa Fluor $\textsuperscript { \textregistered }$ 488 Imaging Kit, Invitrogen). The seedling were washed with PBS buffer for 2 times and analysed by Leica DM IRB epifluorescent microscope with FITC/GFP filter (AlexaFluor 488: excitation $4 9 5 \ \mathrm { n m }$ ; emission $5 1 9 \ \mathrm { n m }$ ). Images were recorded by Leica DFC350 FX camera. 

Metabolites measurement Thiol metabolites were extracted with 0.1 M HCL and separated on a LiChroCART 125x4 mm LiChrospher 60 RP-select column and quantified with the fluorescent dye monobromobimane (Synchem) described by 12. Soluble sugars were extracted by $8 0 \%$ ethanol and separated on Dionex ICS-3000 system with CarboPac PA1 with CarboPac PA1-Guard column at $2 5 \mathrm { { ^ \circ C } }$ . 

Immunological Detection of Proteins Total soluble proteins were extracted from $5 0 ~ \mathrm { m g }$ plant materials with 250 µl 2x Laemmli buffer supplemented with $1 \%$ phosphatase inhibitor cocktail 2 (Sigma). Proteins were denatured for 5 min at $9 5 \mathrm { ^ \circ C }$ and separated on $1 0 \%$ SDS-PAGE. Subsequently, proteins were blotted to nitrocellulose membrane. The primary antibodies anti-S6k-p (Phospho-p70 S6 Kinase (p-Thr389), cell signaling, #9205, 1:5000), eIF-2α Phospho (Epitomics, #1090-1, 1:10,000) and anti-SiR (Khan et al., 2010,1:5000) were detected using the HRP-conjugated secondary antibody (1:30,000). The band intensity was quantified by Image Quant LAS4000 version 1.21 and normalized by the loading control. 

For autophagy detection by protein markers and detection of S6K, proteins were extracted from $5 0 ~ \mathrm { m g }$ materials in $1 0 0 ~ \mu \mathrm { l }$ urea buffer $\mathbf { \Delta } _ { 4 } \mathbf { \Delta } _ { \mathbf { M } }$ urea, $1 0 0 ~ \mathrm { m M }$ DTT and $1 \%$ Triton X-100) and separated on $1 5 \%$ SDS PAGE containing 8 M urea. After separation, proteins were transferred to PVDF membrane. Antibodies against ATG8a-PE (1:2000) and NBR1 (1:5000) were described in 13. 

Pinciple Component Analysis The PCA of metabolites data was performed by MetaboAnalyst 3.0. Data was normalized to WT and log-transformed. Data scale was auto-scaled 14. 

Cell size measurement Cell wall was stained by $0 . 1 ~ \%$ SR 2200 solution $0 . 1 \%$ SR 2200, $1 \%$ DMSO, $0 . 0 5 \%$ Triton-X100, $5 \%$ glycerol, $4 \%$ paraformaldehyde) and immediately analyzed under a confocal scanhead LSM 510 META mounted on an Axiovert 200M 

microscope. Cell size was measured from the cell wall staining along single epidermal cell files. The meristematic zone was defined as the region of isodiametric cells from the QC up to the cell that was twice the length of the immediately preceding cell 7. Cell size was measured by the online-software CellSet (CPIB)8.
