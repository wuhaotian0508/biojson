# Summary

Seed dormancy is an important adaptive trait in plants. Proper seed dormancy enables the avoidance of preharvest sprouting in the undesirable conditions like rainfall frequently. In this study, qPSR8, a major QTL for preharvest sprouting, was isolated, and a previously reported heading-date gene, CCT30, was verified as the candidate gene. The CCT30 knockout mutants (CCT30-CR) enhanced seed dormancy and ABA sensitivity as compared with the wild-type ZH11. Conversely, CCT30 overexpressing plants had opposite phenotype changes and had a decreased ABA content. The expression of ABA synthesis genes such as OsNCEDs and ABA signalling genes such as ABI3 and ABI5 were upregulated and sugar metabolism-related genes such as amylase genes were downregulated in CCT30-CR. Correspondingly, fewer free sugars, such as monosaccharides and oligosaccharides, accumulated in CCT30-CR. The freshly harvested seeds from CCT30-CR had no ability to transmit sugar signals when treated with $1 \%$ exogenous glucose. In addition, CCT30 interacted with the transcription factor OsbZIP37, which negatively regulates seed dormancy. Overall, CCT30 promotes preharvest sprouting by enhancing sugar signals that inhibit the ABA-mediated pathway, and CCT30 is a good gene for breeding rice varieties resistant to preharvest sprouting. 

# Introduction

Seed dormancy is an innate seed property that defines the environmental conditions in which the seed is able to germinate (Finch-Savage and Leubner-Metzger, 2006). The ability of seeds of many plant species to survive until dormancy recedes and to meet the requirements for germination is an adaptive strategy that can act as a buffer against the negative effects of environmental heterogeneity (Klupczynska and Pawłowski, 2021). In persistent rainy and wet weather, mature seeds with low dormancy will germinate directly on the panicle, a phenomenon known as preharvest sprouting (PHS) (Gubler et al., 2005). PHS not only affects crop yield and grain quality, but also affects seed storage and germination rate, causing great economic losses to crop production (Sohn et al., 2021; Tai et al., 2021). The basis of solving the problem of PHS is to analyse the regulatory mechanism of various factors such as environmental factors and internal factors on seed dormancy and germination. 

During embryogenesis, ABA biosynthesis genes and ABA signalling genes are needed for seed maturation, induction of primary dormancy and seed germination (Ali et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2014). In the process of ABA biosynthesis, the catalytic step of 9-cis-epoxidized carotenoid dioxygenase (NCED) is the rate-limiting step (Schwartz et al., 2003). The corresponding 

nced6 nced9 double mutant shows a significant decrease in seed ABA content and concomitantly reduced seed dormancy (Lefebvre et al., 2006). ODR1, whose transcript levels are directly suppressed by the transcription factor ABA INSENSITIVE3 (ABI3), negatively regulates seed dormancy in Arabidopsis by interacting with bHLH57 and inhibiting bHLH57-modulated NCED6 and NCED9 expression (Liu et al., 2020). The bHLH transcription factor seed dormancy 6 (SD6) and inducer of C-repeat binding factor expression 2 (ICE2) antagonistically control seed dormancy in rice by directly regulating the ABA catabolism gene ABA8ox3 and indirectly regulating the ABA biosynthesis gene NCED2 via the SD6/ICE2-OsbHLH048-NCED2 module, in a temperature-dependent manner (Xu et al., 2022). ABA signalling terminator (ABT), a WD40 protein, is induced by ABA in a PYR1/PYL/RCAR-PP2C-dependent manner (Wang et al., 2020c). Overexpression of ABT promotes seed germination and seedling greening in the presence of ABA, whereas knockout of ABT has the opposite effect (Wang et al., 2020c). GA plays at least two types of signalling roles in promoting seed germination (Karssen et al., 1989). First, it mediates the softening of the tissue around the embryo and overcomes the mechanical limit of the seed coat. Second, it promotes the development of seed embryos. The bioactive GA level in plant tissues is determined by the balance between anabolism and catabolism (Yamaguchi, 2008). OsGA20ox2, encoding a GA synthesis enzyme, and OsGA2ox3, 

encoding a GA deactivation enzyme, are candidate genes for the control of seed germination in rice (Magwa et al., 2016; Ye et al., 2015). Gibberellin, low temperature stratification, and hydrogen peroxide treatments activate the MKK3-MPK7 module in Arabidopsis thaliana, releasing seed dormancy by activating the expression of $\mathscr { X }$ -EXPANSIN (EXPAs), a gene associated with cell expansion (Chen et al., 2023a). The regulation of the REVEILLE1 (RVE1) transcription factor to seed dormancy and germination requires a DELLA repressor, REPRESSOR OF GA-LIKE2 (RGL2), in A. thaliana, and the RVE1-RGL2 module coordinately controls seed dormancy and germination by integrating light perception, GA metabolism and GA signalling pathways (Yang et al., 2020). 

ABA induces dormancy, while GA plays a key role in the release of dormancy and germination (Farooq et al., 2022; Shu et al., 2016b). Thus, the balance of ABA/GA levels is a crucial regulatory mechanism for the maintenance and release of seed dormancy. Overexpression of OsNCED3 has been found to enhance PHS resistance by upregulating the ABA/GA ratio to a proper value (Chen et al., 2023b). The DELLA protein in Arabidopsis and the CCT family protein Ghd7 in rice regulate seed dormancy by increasing and decreasing the ratio of ABA to GA, respectively (Hu et al., 2021; Lim et al., 2013). Seed Dormancy4 (Sdr4), a central modulator of seed dormancy, integrates the ABA and GA signalling pathways at the transcriptional level (Zhao et al., 2022). Other endogenous signalling factors such as reactive oxygen species (ROS) and environmental factors such as temperature and light also affect the balance between ABA and GA, ultimately regulating seed dormancy and germination (Tuan et al., 2018). 

Rice seed development is dynamically divided into four stages (An et al., 2020; Tao et al., 2022). The third stage is 20–30 days after fertilization (DAF), which is dominated by the accumulation of starch and proteins in endosperm. The seeds in this stage undergo a major loss of water and the process culminates in a dry and quiescent seed. At this stage, seeds are susceptible to germinate under hot and humid conditions, causing PHS (An et al., 2020). In rice endosperm, multiple starch grains begin in amylosomes and fuse into larger complex structures during seed maturation (Huang et al., 2021). Storage starch is an insoluble granule composed of two $\pmb { \alpha }$ -glucose polymers, linear amylose and amylopectin, linked by a-1, 4-glycosidic and $\mathsf { \Omega } \mathsf { x } ^ { - 1 }$ , 6-glycosidic bonds (Zhu et al., 2020). Complete starch hydrolysis is achieved by the combined action of $\pmb { \alpha }$ -amylase, $\beta \cdot$ -amylase, debranching enzyme, and $\propto$ -glucosidase (Huang et al., 2021). Seed germination and seedling formation are degradation processes of starch stored in endosperm (Tai et al., 2021). The endosperm with high resistance starch has a slower consumption of starch and less production of soluble sugar, thus delaying seed germination (Pan et al., 2018). BZR1 is a key downstream transcription factor mediating BR regulation of seed germination (Xiong et al., 2022). BZR1 affects the expression and activity of a-amylase and starch degradation in endosperm by binding to the promoter of RAmy3D. In addition, studies on barley have shown that impaired starch synthesis in endosperm affects embryo dormancy, possibly because endosperm sugar concentration regulates embryo response to ABA (Howard et al., 2012). PHS8 encodes a starch debranching enzyme, and ABA signalling related to seed dormancy was found to be inhibited with increasing sugar content in phs8 mutants (Du et al., 2018). Soluble sugar accumulated in the endosperm of phs8 mutants downregulates the expression of ABA-responsive genes OsABI3 and 

OsABI5 and decreases sensitivity to ABA, eventually leading to the occurrence of PHS. 

qPSR8 is a major QTL for preharvest sprouting in rice that has been mapped to a 1.5-cM region flanked by markers RM447 and RM3754 on chromosome 8 (Gao et al., 2008). In this study, qPSR8 was isolated by a map-based cloning strategy. qPSR8 encodes a CCT containing protein CCT30. Previous studies have shown overexpression of CCT30 results in a delayed flowering phenotype under both SD and LD conditions and suppresses flowering by promoting Ghd7 and repressing RID1 (Wu et al., 2018). Here, we found that CCT30 interacts with OsbZIP37 to regulate PHS through the ABA-mediated pathway. 

# Results

# Isolation of the major QTL qPSR8 for preharvest sprouting

To narrow down the previously mapped QTL, $q { P S R 8 } ,$ a near isogenic $\mathsf { F } _ { 2 }$ population of 5412 plants was developed by crossing G46B with K7481 to measure the PHS rate (Gao et al., 2008). The PHS rate of G46B was $8 8 . 5 \%$ , while that of K7481 was $7 . 3 \%$ . A total of 1326 plants with PHS rates equal to or greater than $8 5 \%$ (recessive phenotype) were selected from $5 4 1 2 ~ \mathsf { F } _ { 2 }$ plants for genotyping with two SSR markers RM447 and RM3754. A total of 76 recombinants were identified between two markers. These recombinants were further confirmed to be homozygous recessive by progeny test. Two SSR markers (RM23517, RM23520) and six newly designed markers around qPSR8 were used to genotype these recombinants (Table S1). Finally, $q P S R 8$ was subsequently fine-mapped to a $2 8 . 5 – 1 \mathrm { k b }$ fragment between markers P1 and P3 (Figure 1a), and annotation assay showed that the region of the Nipponbare genome (http://rice.uga.edu/) has only one gene, LOC_Os08g42440. It encodes a protein containing a CCT domain at the C terminus and a BBOX domain at the N terminus that was named CCT30 in our previous study (Zhang et al., 2021). 

In addition, the sequence comparison of parental CCT30 alleles showed that the variants including insertion–deletion and SNPs between G46B and K7481 were located in exons or the boundary between exons and introns (Table S2). To investigate whether CCT30 regulates PHS, the complete promoter and gene sequence of CCT30 from G46B were constructed in a complementary vector and transformed into the line NIL-1242 carrying K7481 alleles at CCT30 (Figure 1b). Germination test showed that the seed dormancy degree of the NIL-1242 complementary line was significantly weaker than that of NIL-1242 complementary line seeds (Figure 1c,d). To further verify CCT30 as the candidate gene of qPSR8, CCT30 knockout mutants (CCT30-CR) were generated with a 1-bp insertion, 14-bp deletion or 25-bp deletion at the target site in the ZH11 background (Figure S1). Seeds harvested at 30 DAF from CCT30-CR plants exhibited slower germination than those from wild-type ZH11 (Figure 1e,f). The germination percentages of the CCT30-CR lines were less than $10 \%$ at 12 days after imbibition (Figure 1g). In contrast, the percentage of ZH11 reached $2 6 . 7 \%$ . Unlike CCT30-CR, seeds from CCT30 overexpressing plants showed less ABA sensitivity than the control (Figure S2a). In addition, the germination rate of freshly harvested seeds from the overexpressed CCT30 plants either in Nip or ZH11 backgrounds was higher than that of the controls (Figure 5g,h and Figure S2a). Thus, CCT30 is the gene underlying qPSR8, which negatively regulates seed dormancy.

# Expression pattern of CCT30

CCT30 was highly expressed in seeds at 15 DAF and was mainly expressed in embryos (Figure S4a). The expression of CCT30 was significantly elevated at 6 h after imbibition and reached the highest level at $1 2 \mathrm { ~ h ~ }$ but subsequently and slowly declined (Figure S4b). Furthermore, a germination assay was conducted for the stored seeds, in which dormancy was lost. Compared with the wild-type ZH11, CCT30-CR lines not only exhibited more delayed germination but also had significantly lower germination rates (Figure S5). Therefore, CCT30 not only regulates seed dormancy but also participates in the regulation of seed germination. 

# CCT30 involvement in the ABA pathway

To explore the sensitivity of CCT30 to ABA, seeds harvested at 30 DAF from the wild-type ZH11 and CCT30-CR were treated with 

$5 ~ \mu \mathsf { M }$ ABA. The seed germination of the CCT30-CR was inhibited more strongly than that of ZH11, and the germination rate of CCT30-CR was approximately $1 \%$ at 10 days after treatment with $5 ~ \mu \mathsf { M }$ ABA (Figure 2a,b), indicating that CCT30-CR was more sensitive to ABA than wild-type ZH11. Overexpression of CCT30 decreased ABA sensitivity during seed germination, and the ABA content of the CCT30 overexpressing plants was lower than that of the wild-type plants (Figure S2). Conversely, GA ${ ' } 6 0 0 ~ { \mu \mathsf { M } } )$ had a weak effect on the germination of seeds freshly harvested from ZH11 and had no significant effect on that of seeds from CCT30-CR (Figure S6). In addition, no significant expression regularity in the ABA catabolism pathway genes was observed in the seeds of CCT30-CR at 15 DAF (Figure S7), but the expression of ABA biosynthesis pathway genes and ABA signalling pathway genes were increased compared with that in wild-type seeds (Figure 2c,d). These results support the conclusion that CCT30-CR enhances seed dormancy via the ABA-mediated pathway.

# CCT30 interacts with OsbZIP37

To identify the proteins interacting with CCT30, the self-activation of the full-length CCT30 protein was first examined. Because the complete CCT30 protein has self-activation activity (Figure 4a and Figure S9), two truncated proteins CCT30-B and CCT30-C containing N-terminal BBOX and C-terminal CCT domains, respectively, but without self-activation activity were used as baits to screen the cDNA library via a yeast two-hybrid $( Y _ { 2 } H )$ assay. Finally, a bZIP transcription factor OsbZIP37, a nuclear transcription factor Y subunit C OsNF-YC2 and chlorophyll A-B binding protein were identified. Further $\mathsf { Y } _ { 2 } \mathsf { H }$ 

assays with the truncated OsbZIP37 proteins showed that the interaction has occurred between the domain outside of the bZIP domain of OsbZIP37 and the BBOX domain of CCT30 (Figure 4b). Subsequently, the in vivo interaction between the full-length CCT30 protein and the OsbZIP37 protein in the nucleus was confirmed by BiFC experiments in rice protoplasts and LCI experiments in tobacco (Figure 4c,d). 

# OsbZIP37 negatively regulates seed dormancy

To explore whether OsbZIP37 is also involved in the regulation of seed dormancy, OsbZIP37-overexpressing plants OsbZIP37-OE1 and OsbZIP37-OE2 were generated with different expression levels (Figure 5a and Figure S10). The germination rate of the freshly harvested seeds from these plants had a gradient increase 

with the increase in the expression level of OsbZIP37 (Figure 5c,d). We also generated OsbZIP37 knockout mutants with insertion or deletion (Figure 5b). Seed dormancy was enhanced in OsbZIP37 knockout mutants compared to the negative controls (Figure 5e, f). In conclusion, OsbZIP37 like CCT30 plays a negative regulatory role in rice seed dormancy. 

To evaluate the effects of CCT30 or OsbZIP37 on seed dormancy in different backgrounds and explore the biological significance of the interaction between CCT30 and OsbZIP37, a set of transgenic plants in the ZH11 background was developed (Figure S11). We investigated the dormancy degree of freshly harvested seeds from CCT30 knockout mutants (CCT30-CR), CCT30-overexpressing plants (CCT30-OX), OsbZIP37 knockout mutants (OsbZIP37-CR), OsbZIP37-overexpressing plants (- OsbZIP37-OE) and double mutants simultaneously knocking out OsbZIP37 and overexpressing CCT30 (CCT30-OX/bzip37-cri), which were grown in the same conditions. The germination assay confirmed again that CCT30 and OsbZIP37 negatively regulated rice seed dormancy (Figure 5). Moreover, the germination rates of CCT30-OX/bzip37-cri lines were increased compared with OsbZIP37-CR lines (Figure 5g,h), demonstrating that overexpressing CCT30 rescued the high dormancy caused by OsbZIP37 knock-out. The expression of ABA biosynthesis pathway and ABA signalling pathway genes in these lines were analysed (Figure 5i,j). Compared with the control ZH11, the expression of these genes with a positive effect on seed dormancy such as NCED1, NCED2, NCED3, NCED4, ABI3 and ABI5 was upregulated in the seeds of CCT30-CR and OsbZIP37-CR plants and downregulated in the seeds of CCT30-OX and OsbZIP37-OE plants. While these genes were downregulated in seeds of CCT30-OX/bzip37-cri as that in CCT30-OX, resulting in lower seed dormancy and higher germination rate in CCT30-OX/bzip37- cri (Figure 5i,j). In addition, the expression level of Amy1A, Amy1C and Amy3A genes responsible for starch hydrolysis was lower in CCT30-CR than that in ZH11 (Figure 3d). Similarly, the expression level of Amy1A and Amy3A were simultaneously downregulated in OsbZIP37-CR and upregulated in OsbZIP37-OE (Figure S12). 

# Discussion

# CCT30 negatively regulates ABA biosynthesis and ABA signalling

ABA content is closely associated with seed dormancy. The level of endogenous ABA is determined by the dynamic balance of biosynthesis and catabolism of the hormone (Nambara and Marion-Poll, 2005). Changes in endogenous ABA levels are closely related to the expression of NCED transcripts (Schwartz et al., 2003). In this study, the expression of ABA catabolism genes is not regulated in CCT30 mutants (Figure S4). However, the expression of NCED genes is upregulated in CCT30-CR and downregulated in CCT30-OX plants (Figures 3c and 5j). Consistent with this, ABA content was lower in CCT30-OX than in wildtype ZH11 (Figure S2b). In addition, hormonal signals, especially those of ABA, also play a dominant role in determining whether a seed germinates or not (Gong et al., 2022). As compared with the wild type under ABA treatment, ABA insensitive Arabidopsis mutants such as ABI1 (ABA Insensitive 1), ABI2, ABI3, ABI4 and ABI5 had enhanced germination and seedling growth and exhibited a weaker seed dormancy phenotype (Gong et al., 2022). Similarly, overexpression of OsABI3 or OsABI5 could partially rescue the PHS phenotype of phs8 in rice (Du 

et al., 2018). In this study, CCT30 knockout mutants showed enhanced seed dormancy and increased ABA sensitivity, while CCT30 overexpressing plants showed decreased seed dormancy and ABA sensitivity (Figures 2a,b and 5h and Figure S2a), indicating CCT30 is a negative regulator to the ABA signalling pathway. Besides, the expression of ABI3 and ABI5, key genes in the ABA signalling pathway, were upregulated in CCT30-CR and downregulated in CCT30-OX (Figures 2d and 5i). Thus, it is concluded that CCT30 could negatively regulate ABA signalling pathway. 

# CCT30 negatively regulates seed dormancy through sugar signalling

Sugar, as a source of nutrition, supplies energy to seeds. However, an increasing number of studies suggest that sugar may also act as a signal to regulate seed dormancy and germination during seed maturation. OsPK5, encoding a pyruvate kinase, is a crucial regulator of seed germination in rice (Yang et al., 2022). The disruption of OsPK5 function resulted in slow germination and glucose accumulation and affected the GA/ABA balance. A protection mechanism was conferred by the R2R3 MYB transcription factor carbon starved anther (CSA) during early seed germination by balancing glucose and ABA metabolism to optimize seed germination and stress response fitness (Blochl€ et al., 2007; Sun et al., 2021). A high content of glucose/sucrose stimulates the energy requirements of the sheath, and endosperm sugar is sufficient to suppress the expression of ABA signalling genes to cause preharvest sprouting in phs8 mutant (Du et al., 2018). Therefore, endosperm sugar likely suppresses seed dormancy through ABA signalling. In this study, ABA signalling genes OsABI3 and OsABI5 were upregulated in CCT30 knockout mutants compared with that in wild-type (Figures 2d and 5i). OsGA2ox9 knockout mutants presented obvious preharvest sprouting under successive rainy weather, and OsGA2ox9 prevents GA in embryos from entering the endosperm to inhibit GA-soluble sugar-ABA signalling, ensuring that early seeds (embryo-maturation stage) will not germinate prematurely (Xing et al., 2023). In this study, differentially expressed genes regulated by CCT30 in the seeds at 15 DAF or 30 DAF, the key time points of embryo and endosperm development and seed dormancy establishment, were significantly enriched in starch metabolism, sugar signalling and other related pathways (Figure 3a,b and Figure S7). Moreover, transcription and activity of a-amylases are suppressed and result in low concentrations of soluble sugars in CCT30-CR seeds (Figure 3); in turn, ABA biosynthesis and ABA signalling is promoted (Figure 2c,d). Then, seed dormancy is established, and premature germination is inhibited in CCT30-CR seeds. 

Exogenous $1 \%$ sorbitol treatment had similar inhibitory effects on seed germination for both wildtype and CCT30-CR, indicating osmotic pressure inhibits seed germination for both genotypes (Figure 3e). Previous studies have revealed that different concentrations of exogenous sugars have different effects on seed germination. High concentration of exogenous sugar inhibits germination (Xing et al., 2009; Zhao et al., 2021b). Whereas low concentration of sugar promotes germination (Finkelstein and Lynch, 2000; Garciarrubio et al., 1997). The germination of isolated embryos from the mature phs8 and wildtype seeds showed that the $0 . 6 \%$ glucose or $1 . 5 \%$ glucose in medium promotes embryo germination (Du et al., 2018). In this study, the germination rate of wild-type ZH11 treated with $1 \%$ glucose was slightly higher than that treated with water (Figure 3e). No studies

# CCT30 and OsbZIP37 synergistically regulate a series of genes influencing seed dormancy

Basic leucine zip-proteins (bZIPs) are an evolutionarily conserved family of transcription factors that regulate diverse plant-specific phenomena, including seed dormancy and germination, floral induction, and photomorphogenesis (Droge-Laser € et al., 2018; Jakoby et al., 2002; Nijhawan et al., 2008; Sornaraj et al., 2016). Both ABI3 and ABI5 encode bZIP domain proteins that play important roles in ABA signalling and seed germination in Arabidopsis (Lopez-Molina et al., 2002). bZIP67 induces dormancy by binding to the GBL cis-element on the DOG1 promoter and upregulating its expression during the development of Arabidopsis seeds (Bryant et al., 2019). OsbZIP72 regulates seed germination through ABA-JA synergistic interactions (Wang et al., 2020b). OsMFT2 positively regulates ABA-responsive genes by interacting with OsbZIP23/66/72 and inhibits seed germination (Song et al., 2020). Here, the interaction between OsbZIP37 and CCT30 was verified by $Y _ { 2 } H$ , BiFC and LCI assays (Figure 4). The dormancy of seeds overexpressing OsbZIP37 was weakened (Figure 5), while the seed dormancy of the OsbZIP37 knockout mutant was enhanced, suggesting that OsbZIP37 negatively regulates seed dormancy. 

OsbZIP37 is a transcriptional activator. Transcriptome analysis showed that a total of 2268 genes were upregulated in the OsbZIP37ox cells (Miyamoto et al., 2014). In this study, a combined analysis of RNA-seq data identified 26 and 31 downstream genes coregulated by CCT30 and OsbZIP37 at 15 DAF and 30 DAF, respectively. These genes were upregulated in OsbZIP37-overexpressing cells and downregulated in CCT30-CR mutant seeds (Table S3). Functional annotation revealed that these genes are involved in seed development, energy metabolism and hormone signalling responses. Some genes encode ATPase, glycosyl hydrolase and a-amylase. For example, OsTPS1 encodes trehalose 6-phosphate synthetase, a key enzyme in trehalose biosynthesis (Table S3). OsTPS1 is induced by drought, high salinity, low temperature, and ABA treatment (Kim et al., 2005). OsPHY1 was highly expressed in endosperm and hypocotyl of germinated seeds and responded to abscisic acid, gibberellin and indoleacetic acid (Guo et al., 2013; Li et al., 2012). OsDOG1L-3 promotes seed dormancy by enhancing ABA synthesis, and OsbZIP75 and OsbZIP78 induce OsDOG1L-3 expression by directly binding to its promoter and promoting seed dormancy (Wang et al., 2020a). Moreover, except OsPHY1, the expression levels of OsTPS1 and OsDOG1L-3 were upregulated in CCT30-CR and OsbZIP37-CR, while downregulated in 

CCT30-OX and OsbZIP37-OE. In addition, the expression level of CCT30-OX/bzip37-cri was similar to that of ZH11 (Figure S13). This suggested that CCT30 regulates seed dormancy and germination by interacting with OsbZIP37 to synergistically regulate the expression of these genes. However, whether these genes are directly bound by CCT30 and OsbZIP37 remains to be further verified. 

# Flowering genes link flowering and seed dormancy in Arabidopsis and rice

ABA not only plays an important role in regulating seed dormancy and germination, but also affects plant flowering. In Arabidopsis, knockout mutants of ABA signalling components such as ABI4 exhibit an earlier flowering phenotype, whereas their overexpression lines show the opposite, indicating the inhibitory effect of ABA signalling on plant flowering (Shu et al., 2016a). AtU2AF65b regulates ABA-governed flowering repression by controlling the precursor messenger RNA splicing of ABI5 and FLC in Arabidopsis (Xiong et al., 2019). Apart from flowering time, the florigen gene Flowering Locus $T ( F T )$ also negatively controls seed dormancy by the transduction of maternal temperature (Chen et al., 2014). Ectopic expression of $F T$ and TFL1 in seeds enhances dormancy by affecting the GA and DOG1 pathways (Chen et al., 2021). The florigen FT binds to either the $3 ^ { \prime }$ region or $5 ^ { \prime }$ region of the floral repressor FLC to repress FLC expression, promotes flowering time and reduces seed dormancy in Arabidopsis (Chen and Penfield, 2018). Similarly, this case was also observed in CCT30. It delays heading date by upregulating Ghd7 and downregulating RID1 (Wu et al., 2018). CCT30 was here confirmed as a negative regulator of dormancy (Figure 1). In contrast, the heading-date gene Ghd7 acted as a negative regulator of germination. The ghd7 mutants showed a low sensitivity to exogenous ABA and a low expression of OsNCED genes in the process of seed germination (Hu et al., 2021). Exogenous ABA inhibits flowering via a “SAPK8-ABF1- Ehd1/Ehd2” pathway in rice, in which the ABA signalling component SnRK2 kinase SAPK8 phosphorylates the bZIP type transcription factor ABF1 to enhance its direct repression of Ehd1 and Ehd2, and eventually delay flowering (Tang et al., 2023). These studies indicated that flowering genes link flowering and seed dormancy, two key events in the plant life cycle. 

# Materials and methods

# The population for fine mapping of qPSR8

An $\mathsf { F } _ { 2 }$ population was generated from a cross between the G46B and Lemont cultivars, and the qPSR8 locus was initially mapped to a region delimited by markers RM447 and RM3754 on chromosome 8 (Gao et al., 2008). A line, K7481, was identified from the BC F population with G46B as the recurrent parent and Lemont as the donor parent. K7481 that carries one QTL for PHS 

(qPSR8) and possesses high resistance to PHS, was backcrossed to G46B, and a large near isogenic $\mathsf { F } _ { 2 }$ population of 5412 plants was developed for fine mapping qPSR8. 

# Vector construction and generation of transgenic plants

The plants overexpressing CCT30 (CCT30-OE) and the wild-type Nipponbare were kindly provided by Professor Shihua Cheng from the China Rice Research Institute. The CDS of OsbZIP37 (LOC_Os04g54474) was amplified from cDNA of Zhonghua 11 (ZH11) and inserted into the overexpression vector 1301U to generate the vector OsbZIP37-OE. The CCT30 knockout vector CCT30-CR and OsbZIP37 knockout vector OsbZIP37-CR were constructed using a multitarget CRISPR/Cas9 vector system (Ma et al., 2015). First, the target sequence was designed online (http://crispr.hzau.edu.cn/CRISPR2/) (Lei et al., 2014) and amplified by PCR. Then, it was introduced into sgRNA expression cassettes driven by the promoters OsU6a (Table S1). Then, the sgRNA expression cassettes were ligated into the binary vector pCXUN-CAS9 using the Golden Gate cloning method (Engler et al., 2008, 2009). CRISPR and overexpression vectors were introduced into the callus of cultivar ZH11 via Agrobacteriummediated transformation (Lin and Zhang, 2005). These CCT30 transgenic plants were grown in the experimental field of Huazhong Agricultural University in Wuhan from 2020 to 2022, and $O s b Z I P 3 7 \top _ { 0 }$ transgenic plants were grown in 2022. 

To generate the CCT30-OX/bzip37-cri and OsbZIP37-OX/cct30- cri constructs, simultaneously knocking out OsbZIP37 and overexpressing CCT30 or simultaneously knocking out CCT30 and overexpressing OsbZIP37, the pCXUN-Cas9 vector was modified by inserting a CaMV 35S promoter behind the Cas9 sequence (Song et al., 2020). This vector was introduced into the callus of cultivar ZH11 via Agrobacterium-mediated transformation, and then CCT30 knockout mutants (CCT30-CR), zCCT30-overexpressing plants $( C C T 3 0 – O X )$ , OsbZIP37 knockout mutants (OsbZIP37-CR), OsbZIP37-overexpressing plants (- OsbZIP37-OE) and double mutants simultaneously knocking out OsbZIP37 and overexpressing CCT30 (CCT30-OX/bzip37-cri) with the ZH11 background were generated (Figure S11). These ${ \sf T } _ { 0 }$ transgenic plants were grown in the experimental field of Yazhouwan National Laboratory in Sanya in 2024. 

The genotype of CCT30 in the line NIL-1242 is consistent with that of K7481. To generate a complementary line of NIL-1242, the complete promoter and gene sequence of CCT30 of G46B was amplified and constructed to a complementary vector. Then it was transformed into NIL-1242. These T0 transgenic plants were grown in the experimental field of Yazhouwan National Laboratory in Sanya in 2024. 

# PHS investigation

All plants of the large near isogenic F2 population planted in the Chengdu experimental station of Sichuan Academy of Agricultural Sciences in 2012 were labelled with heading date and harvested 35 days after flowering. The main panicle (approximately 5 cm from the base of the ear) of each plant was harvested and completely immersed in water for $4 \textrm { h }$ . A layer of plastic film was spread in the greenhouse, and then the panicles were spread flat on the film, covered with a layer of film, sprayed with the appropriate amount of water, and sealed at $3 0 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathsf C$ constant temperature for 7 days to calculate the germination rate. For further PHS confirmation in the progeny, 3 plants for each recombinant line were randomly selected and the main 

panicles were tested following the protocol described by Gao et al. (2008). 

# Seed dormancy comparison and germination assay

For the seed dormancy assay, the panicles of the WT and transgenic lines were tagged at the start of fertilization and harvested at after 30 days. Then, the fresh seeds were soaked in water, $5 ~ { \mu \mathrm { m } }$ ABA solution or other solutions, and placed in a growth chamber at $3 0 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathsf C$ in the dark. Germination was scored visually when the radicle was ≥1 mm in length. These assays were replicated three times, and in every replicate, at least 100 seeds of each line were used. 

# RNA extraction

Fresh leaves were ground into a fine powder and then used for total RNA extraction with TRIzol reagent (TransGen Biotech, Beijing) according to the manufacturer’s protocol. RNA extraction of rice seeds followed a published protocol (Wang et al., 2012), with some modifications. Briefly, seeds at different developmental stages were ground to fine powder with a mortar and pestle in liquid nitrogen and transferred to an RNase-free tube containing $4 0 0 ~ \mu \iota$ of RNA extraction buffer (100 mmol ${ \mathsf { L } } ^ { - 1 }$ Tris–HCl (pH 9.0), $2 \%$ $\beta$ -ME (v/v)), then mixed thoroughly in a vortexer. A volume of $2 0 ~ \mu \iota$ of $20 \%$ SDS was added to the suspension, that was inverted gently and centrifuged at $1 2 \ 0 0 0 \times \pmb { g }$ for 10 min at $4 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathsf C$ g. The supernatant was transferred to a new tube containing $0 . 8 ~ \mathsf { m L }$ of TRIzol (TransGen Biotech, Beijing, China) and mixed thoroughly. RNA in the mixture was extracted with chloroform and precipitated with isopropanol following the manufacturer’s instructions. 

# Real-time PCR and analysis of RNA-seq data

For real-time PCR, $4 ~ \mu \ g$ of total RNA was digested with DNase I and reverse transcribed with an M-MLV reverse transcriptase kit (Invitrogen) according to the manufacturer’s protocol. Real-time PCR was performed in a 384-well plate in a QuantStudio 6 Flex real-time PCR system (Applied Biosystems) using FastStart Universal SYBR Green Master Mix (Roche, CH). The relative expression level of the target gene compared to that of the internal reference gene ubiquitin was calculated. The primers used for real-time PCR are listed in Table S1. 

For RNA-seq analysis, total RNA was subjected expression profiling analysis using the Illumina sequencing platform. DEGs (differentially expressed genes) were identified when $P < 0 . 0 5$ and log2 FoldChange $> 1$ for RNA-seq data. The KEGG enrichment was estimated by using custom R scripts. 

# Measurement of soluble sugar content

Sugars were extracted from freshly harvested seeds of CCT30-CR and the wild type. Each sample had three replicates. Seeds were ground into powder with liquid nitrogen, and 100 mg of powder was extracted with $1 0 0 0 ~ \mu \iota$ of $70 \%$ methanol overnight at $4 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathsf C$ (Chen et al., 2013). 

# Yeast two-hybrid assay

For $\mathsf { Y } _ { 2 } \mathsf { H }$ screening, the CDS of CCT30 was fused in frame with pGBKT7. The plasmid pGADT7 was used as bait for screening the cDNA library. To verify the interaction between CCT30 and OsbZIP37 in yeast, different portions of their CDSs were fused with pGBKT7 or pGADT7 and then cotransformed into the yeast strain AH109. The transformants were grown on SD/ Leu/ Trp 

medium and then transferred to SD/ Leu/ Trp/–His/ Ade medium to detect the interaction. 

# BiFC assay

For the BiFC assay, the CDSs of CCT30 and OsbZIP37 were amplified from cDNA of ZH11 and cloned and inserted into vectors PVYNE and PVYCE, respectively. The combinations of plasmids were transformed into rice protoplasts. After incubation in the dark for $1 2 { - } 1 6 \mathrm { ~ h ~ }$ , fluorescence was observed by confocal microscopy (Leica Microsystems, https://www.leica-microsystems. com). 

# Luciferase complementation imaging assay

LCI experiments were performed as previously described (Chen et al., 2008). The CDSs of CCT30 and OsbZIP37 were amplified and integrated into vectors pCAMBIA-NLUC and pCAMBIA-CLUC. Each construct was transformed into A. tumefaciens strain EHA105. All constructs were coexpressed in 5-week-old Nicotiana benthamiana leaves by Agrobacterium-mediated transformation. After 2 days, 1 mM luciferin was applied to the leaves and then detected using a 5200 Chemiluminescent Imaging System (Tanon Science and Technology). 

# Determination of ABA content

The seeds of CCT30 overexpressing plants and ZH11 were collected and stored at $- 8 0 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathsf C$ for ABA quantification. The ABA contents were determined by Webiolotech Testing Technology Co., Ltd. (Nanjing, China) based on ESI-HPLC-MS/MS analysis (http://www.webiolotech.com). 

# Haplotype and nucleotide diversity analysis

The haplotypes of CCT30 were determined using the public genotype data of 4726 rice accessions downloaded from RiceVarMap V2.0 (http://ricevarmap.ncpgr.cn) (Zhao et al., 2021a). SNP and InDel data located in CDS region were extracted for analysis, and only haplotypes with more than 100 accessions $( > 2 \% )$ were selected.
