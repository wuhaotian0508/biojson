# The mutation of ent‑kaurenoic acid oxidase, a key enzyme involved in gibberellin biosynthesis, confers a dwarf phenotype to cucumber

# Abstract

Key message A dwarf mutant with short branches (csdf) was identifed from EMS-induced mutagenesis. Bulked segregant analysis sequencing and map-based cloning revealed CsKAO encoding ent-kaurenoic acid oxidase as the causal gene.

Abstract Plant architecture is the primary target of artifcial selection during domestication and improvement based on the determinate function for fruit yield. Plant architecture is regulated by complicated genetic networks, more underlying mechanism remains to be elucidated. Here, we identifed a dwarf mutant (csdf) in an EMS-induced cucumber population, and genetic analysis revealed the mutated phenotype is controlled by a single recessive gene. Optical microanalysis showed the decrease in cell length is mainly contribute to the dwarf phenotype. By strategy of BSA-seq combined with map-based cloning, CsaV3_6G006520 (CsKAO) on chromosome 6 was identifed as the candidate gene for csdf. Gene cloning and sequence alignment revealed a G to A mutation in the sixth exon, which causes the premature stop codon in CsKAO of csdf. Expression analysis revealed CsKAO was expressed in various tissues with abundant transcripts, and has signifcant differences between WT and csdf. Gene annotation indicated $C s K A O$ encodes a cytochrome P450 family ent-kaurenoic acid oxidase which functioned in GA biosynthesis. GA-relevant analysis showed that endogenous GA contents were signifcantly decreased and the dwarfsm phenotype could be restored by exogenous $\mathrm { G A } _ { 3 }$ treatment; while, some of the representative enzyme genes involved in the GA pathway were up-regulated in csdf. Besides, IAA content is decreased in the terminal bud and increased in the lateral bud in csdf as well as several IAA-related genes are diferentially expressed. Overall, those fndings suggest that CsKAO regulated plant height via the infuence on GAs pathways, and IAA might interact with GAs on plant architecture morphogenesis in cucumber.

# Introduction

Plant architecture displayed species-specifc characteristics and plasticity to external environmental conditions, suitable plant architecture played pivotal efects in increasing crop yield and simplifying cultivation management; thus, it was strongly selected during domestication (Liu et al. 2021). The plant ontogeny is shaped through the progressive addition of various organs and determines plant architecture, this remarkable plasticity is guaranteed by hormone pathways. Plant hormones, as small molecules derived from various metabolic pathways, arrange plant intrinsic developmental systems (Depuydt and Hardtke 2011). Multiple phytohormones (BRs, GAs, SLs, IAAs) have been characterized to regulate plant architecture in diverse phenomena in processes related to plant height, branching, leave angle and foral organ development (Luo et al. 2016; Wang et al. 2018, 2020, 2021; Shen et al. 2019; Pawełkowicz et al. 2019). Plant hormones can either act independently or crosstalk or overlap in diferent biological and cellular processes (Collett et al. 2000; Vanstraelen and Benková 2012; Ross and Quittenden 2016; Street et al. 2016; Tian et al. 2018; Liu et al. 2019).

GAs, a class of growth-promoting hormones refer to over 130 kinds of tetracyclic diterpenoid compounds, which were presented to promote organ growth through enhancing cell elongation or division and activated developmental processes including seed germination, plant height, reproductive organ development, stomatal development as well as disease resistance (Saibo et al. 2003; Hedden and Sponsel 2015; Li et al. 2020; Ding et al. 2021). Bioactive GAs are synthesized and functioned via a multi-step catalytic process with various oxidation–reduction enzymes (Yamaguchi 2008), and the defects of those key enzymes lead to the typical GA-defcient dwarf phenotype in various crop species (Sun and Kamiya 1994; Dalmadi et al. 2008; Plackett et al. 2012; Chen et al. 2014; Li et al. 2021; Yan et al. 2021). The eruption of the Green Revolution relies on the stem elongation function of GAs, which was succeeded by mutations in GAs biosynthesis and signaling genes that acted as the basis of semidwarf rice and wheat, respectively (Peng et al. 1999; Sasaki et al. 2002). In addition to the well-known regulation of plant height, GAs have been reported to have pleiotropic efects on the process of axillary bud outgrowth of diferent species. GAs negatively impacted shoot branching in Arabidopsis, rice, tomato, and Medicago truncatula; while, it was proposed to promote axillary bud outgrowth synergistically with CKs in Jatropha curcas (Ni et al. 2015; Martinez-Bello et al. 2015; Liao et al. 2019; Zhang et al. 2020a, 2020b). Those researches point out that genes associated with GA signals are valuable for the improvement of plant architecture in crop species.

Cucumber is one of the most widely cultivated economic Cucurbitaceae vegetables in the world and owns the typical annual creeping or climbing habit and monopodial branching mode. The cucumber plant architecture is composed of stems, leaves, branches, tendrils, fowers and fruits owing to the characteristics of the continuous vegetable growth and reproductive growth during almost the entire growth life cycle of cucumber (Leonard 1962). Plant height and shoot branching are important components that determine the build of plant architecture; while, dwarf plant statue is more suitable for cucumber protected cultivation and branches infuence the growth of the main stem as well as the mode of manual management. Optimizing plant statues is an efcient approach for breeders to increase efciency and yield and some phytohormones such as GAs, BRs and IAAs play a crucial role in plant growth. At present, several kinds of dwarf mutants have been reported in cucumber, including compact (cp, cp-1, cp-2, cp-3, cpa, cpa-2,), super compact (scp-1, scp-2, scp-3), short internode (si, si-2), dwarf (dw2, csdw) mutants (Li et al. 2011a; Hou et al. 2017; Wang et al. 2017; Xu et al. 2018; Zhang et al. 2021; Anarjan et al. 2023; Sun et al. 2024). BRs and GAs played the main roles on dwarf phenotype. Among them, scp-1, scp-2, scp-3, cpa, cpa-2 displayed the typical BR-defcient dwarf phenotype with dark-green and wrinkle leaves, less branches while sterile female fowers which keeps the fruit from normally expanding, and the candidate genes for scp-1, scp-2, scp-3, cpa, cpa-2 were demonstrated participated in BRs biosynthesis. On account of that BRs play a critical role in inducing parthenocarpic growth in the early growth of fruits in cucumber, it is more difcult to use the BR-related mutants for manipulating plant architecture. cp, cp-3, Csdw, si-2 were reported involving the GAs pathway, CsERECTA controls stem elongation by regulating GA biosynthesis, and diferent SNPs happened in CsERECTA account for cp and cp-3 mutant, respectively. si-2 was caused by the mutation of GA3ox promoter, which subsequently infuence GA signaling pathway. Csdw, which identifed with dwarf phenotype owing to the reduction of cell diferentiation, was sensitive to endogenous GA3 (Xu et al. 2018). Besides, GAs also have other regulatory efects of plant morphogenesis on cucumber except plant height. It was reported that the silence of GID1, which is involved in the GA signal pathway was identifed to afect locule formation in cucumber (Liu et al. 2016). Besides, a variety of species in Cucurbitaceae have been reported involved in plant regulation by GAs pathways. The mutation in Cldf in watermelon which encodes gibberellin $3 \beta$ -hydroxylase in GA biosynthesis leads to the compact architecture with smaller leaves and diferent trichome densities of ovaries and petals of male fowers (Wei et al. 2019). The mutation in GA3ox in pumpkin also confers a dwarf phenotype (Min et al. 2022). The well-known “green revolution” in other proteins (gibberellin 20-oxidase-like protein)

in watermelon also been identifed played a vital role in regulating plant height (Dong et al. 2018). The gibberellin 2-oxidase gene $C p D w$ is involved in a dwarf phenotype in squash (Ding et al. 2021). Thus, GAs hold the high potential in plant-type improvement; while, the molecular mechanism of GAs underlying the plant architecture in cucumber remains largely unknown.

In this study, we identified a novel dwarf cucumber mutant with short branches, csdf, whose mutation is attributed to a recessive gene, from an ethyl methane sulfonate (EMS) mutagenesis population. By using the strategy combining BSA-seq and map-based cloning, we identifed CsaV3_6G006520, which is located on chromosome 6 and encodes cytochrome P450 family ent-kaurenoic acid oxidase involved in GA biosynthesis, was served as the mostly causal gene underlying the dwarfsm phenotype in csdf. Further GA correction analysis and RNA-seq results confrmed that the defciency of GAs in cucumber caused dwarfsm. This is the frst report about dwarf and multiple branches plant architecture directly correlating with cucumber GA biosynthesis. These results provide more novel insights that can facilitate our apprehension of the mechanism that GA functioned in the morphogenesis of plant architecture in cucumber.

# Material and methods

# Plant materials and growth conditions

The Cucumis sativus dwarfsm (csdf) mutant was isolated from the EMS-induced mutagenesis population of Changchunmici (CCMC, an ordinary inbred material). Two distinct $\mathrm { F } _ { 2 }$ populations were developed from crosses between csdf and two inbred lines (CCMC and Hazerd) that with normal plant height for inheritance analysis and causal gene mapping. Seeds of CCMC and csdf were sown and placed in a controlled environment chamber for measurements of phenotypic traits and hormone response experiment at the seedling stage $1 6 \mathrm { h }$ light photoperiod at $2 8 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ and 8 h dark at $2 2 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ ). Other plants cultivated in the greenhouse in Baima of Nanjing Agricultural University, Nanjing, China.

# Histomorphological observation

The second internodes of the main stem from CCMC and csdf at the fowering stage (twelve leave stage) were harvested for scanning electron microscope (SEM). The SEMs were conducted based on the detailed steps described previously (Song et al. 2022). The cell length was measured by Image J.

The second leaves of csdf and WT in the fowering stage were harvested for chlorophyll content measurement using the methods published previously (Song et al. 2018).

# Candidate gene identifcation by MutMap combined with map‑based cloning

A modified MutMap method was used to identify the causal gene for csdf. The DNAs of 24 csdf individuals and 25 wild type plants from the $\mathrm { F } _ { 2 }$ population were extracted by CTAB method and then were mixed in equal ratios to construct a mutant pool and wild pool, respectively. Those two DNA pools were re-sequenced using Illumina HiSeq 2000 (350 bp). All clean reads were aligned onto cucumber ‘Chinese Long’ reference genome sequence (V3) (Li et al. 2019) by Burrows–Wheeler Aligner (BWA) (Li and Richard 2010). The software SAMTools was used for transforming SAM/BWA fles and calling for SNPs (Li et al. 2009). The detection of SNPs and insertion/deletions was measured using GTAK toolkit and all SNP annotations were analyzed by ANNOVAR (Kai et al. 2010). We calculated SNP index, $\Delta$ (SNP index) and $p$ -values to predict the candidate region. Then we focused on the key SNPs that meet the following two points: nonsynonymous mutations or stop-loss, or stopgain, or splice site variants; the mutations of C-T or GA in the mutant pool.

To ensure the accuracy of the candidate gene, we also conducted the positional mapping with another $\mathrm { F } _ { 2 }$ population of hazerd × csdf. Indel and SSR markers were developed based on the re-sequenced results of CCMC and hazerd. We employed a bulked segregation (BSA) analysis strategy to identify csdf locus by csdf -bulk and WT-bulk, and those two pools contain equal ratio DNA of 8 individuals from mutants and WT plants, respectively, the marker were screened where the band type of csdf -bulk was consistent with the parent band of csdf. After initial chromosome anchoring of the csdf locus, new fanking markers were adopted to genotype a population with 205 individuals. Subsequently, with the use of a larger $\mathrm { F } _ { 2 }$ population of 827 plants and 4 new polymorphic markers among the initial interval, csdf was ultimately narrowed down to the candidate region according to the recombinants. The SNP-0 was developed with casual SNP in BSA-seq for linkage analysis. Polymorphisms of Indel and SSR makers were screened by polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis, and SNP marker was detected by Sanger sequencing. The Primer sequences of the polymorphic markers are listed in Supplementary Table S1.

# RNA extraction and qRT‑PCR analysis

Various tissues from diverse cucumber lines were collected at the fowering stage, immediately frozen in liquid nitrogen and subsequently stored at $- 8 0 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ . Each sample has three independent biological replications with at least three individuals per biological replicate. Total RNA was extracted with an RNA Isolation Kit according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Complementary DNA was performed using

a Prime Script™ RT Reagent Kit (TaKaRa). RT-qPCR was conducted with TB-GREEN Premix Ex Taq™ Kit (TaKaRa). CsaV3_6G041900 (CsACTIN2) was acted as the reference gene for normalizing the expression results. The primers were designed by software Primer Premier 5. The primers are listed in Supplementary Table S1. Relative mRNA expression data were calculated using the $2 ^ { - \Delta \Delta C _ { \mathrm { T } } }$ method.

# Phylogenetic analysis and alignment of protein sequences

Sequences of KAO homologs in different species were downloaded from the NCBI database (http://www.ncbi. nlm.nih.gov). DNAMAN software was adapted for multiple alignments of the sequences. The phylogenetic tree was performed using the neighbor-joining method by MEGA with 1000 bootstrap replications.

# Sub‑cellular localization

The full length of CsKAO cDNA without stop codon from WT and csdf were cloned into 35S::GFP to generate the 35S:: CsKAO-GFP and 35S:: CsKAO-M-GFP construct. The 35S::GFP was designated as the control. All vectors were separately infected into tobacco leaves (Nicotiana benthamiana), and then were incubated in the dark at $2 8 ~ ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ for $^ { 7 2 \mathrm { ~ h ~ } }$ , and fnally examined by LSM800 laser Scanning Confocal Microscope.

Endogenous GAs $\mathrm { G A } _ { 1 }$ , $\mathrm { G A } _ { 3 }$ , $\mathrm { G A } _ { 4 }$ , $\mathrm { G A } _ { 1 2 } ,$ ) contents were determined with the leaves from CCMC and csdf, and the shoot apex in the main stem and the branches were sampled for the assays -of endogenous hormones including IAA (Indole-3-acetic acid), IBA (Indole 3-butyric acid), TZR (Trans Zeatin Riboside), Zeatin, all those measurements were conducted by the method of liquid chromatographytandem mass spectrometry. The second leaves and shoot apexes of CCMC and csdf which were grown under the same condition were harvested at the fowering stage and immediately frozen in liquid nitrogen, after which the leaves were ground to powder and extracted with $1 0 ~ \mathrm { m L }$ acetonitrile at $4 4 ^ { \circ } \mathrm { C }$ for $1 2 \mathrm { h }$ . Each sample has three independent biological replications with three individuals per biological replicate. The extraction and content determination were conducted by commercial company Nanjing Webiolotech Biotechnology.

$\mathrm { G A } _ { 3 }$ was frst dissolved with a little ethanol and then diluted with $\mathrm { d d H } _ { 2 } \mathrm { O }$ to the suitable concentration as storage solutions $( 1 0 0 ~ \mathrm { m M } )$ ), and further diluted to the fnal conductive concentrations ( $\mathrm { 0 . 1 \ m M }$ and $0 . 0 1 \ \mathrm { m M }$ ). # RNA‑seq analysis

Total RNA was extracted from stems of CCMC and csdf at the fowering stage in three biological replicates (three individuals were mixed into one biological sample) by Trizol. RNA integrity was determined by Agilent 2100 Bioanalyzer. Transcriptome sequencing was performed with Illumina HiSeq by commercial services (Metware Biotechnology Co., Ltd., Wuhan, China). After fltering out the adapter sequences and low-quality reads, the clean reads were mapped to the cucumber (Chinese Long) V3 genome. Transcript abundance was estimated with FPKM (fragments per kilobase of transcript sequence per million base pairs sequenced) by Stringtie (https://ccb.jhu.edu/software/strin gtie/). Diferentially expressed genes (DEGs) were analyzed and identifed according to the criterion that padj $< 0 . 0 5$ and $| \mathrm { l o g } _ { 2 } ^ { \mathrm { ( F o l d C h a n g e ) } } | > 1$ . Besides, GO (gene ontology) and KEGG (Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes) enrichment analysis of DEGs were conducted by cluster profler based on the hypergeometric distribution principle (Love et al. 2014).

# Results

# The isolation and characterization of csdf mutant

csdf mutant which displayed extreme dwarf state was identifed from an EMS-induced mutagenesis population of CCMC. $\mathrm { F } _ { 1 }$ plants from the cross $( \mathbf { C C M C } \times c s d f )$ share similar phenotypes of wild type in both plant height and average branch length. The phenotypic segregation ratio of $\mathrm { F } _ { 2 }$ (159 WT and 46 mutants) was close to 3:1 $( \chi ^ { 2 } = 0 . 7 2 )$ , and in the backcross $( \mathrm { F } _ { 1 } \times c s d f )$ populations, the separation ratio (97 WT and 85 mutants) was by 1:1 $( \chi ^ { 2 } = 0 . 7 9 )$ . Hence, we concluded that the mutant trait was controlled by the loss of function of a recessive nuclear gene.

The dwarf phenotype of csdf mutant was observed throughout the whole growth cycle. Compared with CCMC, csdf displayed pleiotropic defections in plant architecture. csdf displayed wrinkled cotyledon, the smaller leaves,

shorter ovaries and fruits (Fig. S1), and the hypocotyl length, and plant height of csdf were signifcantly decreased at the mature stage (Fig. 1a–d), and the length and number of branches also decreased signifcantly (Fig. S2). Plant height is determined by the assembly combination of internode numbers and internode length. By feld observation, we revealed that the short stems of csdf were assembled by the signifcantly shortened internode lengths as well as the fewer internode numbers (Fig. 1i). SEM section observations of the second internodes revealed that the internode cell length of csdf was signifcantly shortened than that of WT (Fig. 1e, f, j). Then we calculated both total epidermal cell numbers (internode length /epidermal cell length) of WT and csdf, which clarifed there was no signifcant decrease in cell numbers of csdf (Fig. 1k). These results suggest that the shortening of internode length in csdf mutant is mainly due to the impaired cell elongation. As opposed to the growth of aerial parts of cucumber, there was no big diference in root length in cotyledon stage between WT and csdf seedlings (Fig. 1a). To investigate the reason for the dark-green leaves of csdf, we determined the chlorophyll content, and results revealed that there was no big diference in the pigment content between csdf and WT, which might be attributed to the change in leaf morphology of csdf (Fig. S3).

# Fine mapping and identifcation of CsKAO

Those polymorphic markers were subsequently adopted with BSA strategy to quickly screen the linkage marker of csdf-bulk. BSA strategy revealed that two Indel markers Mut6-2 and Mut6-3 were possessed polymorphism in two bulks, and both of them located on chromosome 6. Then more polymorphic markers among the interval covering these two markers were developed and genotyped a small $\mathrm { F } _ { 2 }$ population with 205 individuals, and the initial mapping delimited csdf locus to a 1.4 Mb region fanking by two indel markers (Mut6-8–3 and Mut6-8–6) based on the recombinants (Fig. 2a). To narrow down the candidate region, a larger $\mathrm { F } _ { 2 }$ population with 827 individuals were examined with 4 more indel markers. Finally, csdf was mapped to a $4 1 \mathrm { k b }$ physical region fanked by markers Mut6-2 and Mut 6–21 on chromosome 6, and six genes were distributed among this region (Fig. 2b, c).

# CsKAO encodes ent‑kaurene acid oxidase that mediates plant architecture

To identify the CsaV3_6G006520 coding sequence, we cloned the full-length with the cDNA from CCMC and csdf of CsaV3_6G006520, and the amplifcation products were conducted by Sanger sequencing. The result indicated that a mutation of G to A happened in the sixth exon of csdf and caused the premature stop codon (Fig. 3). The gene annotation of the CsaV3_6G006520 revealed that it encodes the ent-kaurene acid oxidase. This gene is the homologous of the Arabidopsis KAO gene, so we named as CsKAO. # Mutation of csdf displayed the abnormal axillary bud outgrowth and altered the levels of auxins in the shoot apex

As well as the dwarf phenotype, csdf displayed the altered branching pattern. Here, we revealed that csdf has the normal diferentiation ability, which suggests that CsKAO did not interfere with the initiation of axillary meristems. In contrast, when compared with WT, normal lateral branch

number in the csdf was signifcantly reduced, and the average length of the lower branches in csdf was shorter (Fig. 8b, d), indicating that CsKAO could be involved in the process of axillary bud development. To investigate whether the limited branching of csdf was caused by the decreased GAs concentrations, we recorded the response of lateral branches in csdf and WT with exogenous $\mathrm { G A } _ { 3 }$ . In the csdf, compared with the control, the branch number and branch length were signifcantly increased in plants with $\mathrm { G A } _ { 3 }$ treatment. While, for WT, the branch number and elongation length were signifcantly lower in $\mathrm { G A } _ { 3 }$ treatment compared with that in controls (Fig. 8b, d). These results indicate that GA treatment rescued the mutant phenotype of csdf by promoting axillary bud outgrowth, but the GAs in excess of the physiology concentration repressed lateral branch elongation. Those observations support the notion that the mutation of CsKAO results in a decrease in GAs contents, thereby afecting the axillary bud outgrowth in cucumber (Wang et al. 2017).

# Discussion

Plant architecture has a great infuence on yield potential, vegetable quality and cultivation management mode via determining plant resource allocation, the characteristics of labor-saving and high yield are the prerequisites for breeding new varieties of the ideal plant architecture of cucumber. As the vital agronomic factors, plant height and lateral branching directly determine the shoot architecture, hold

great potential for labor-saving cultivar development and afect crop yield and labor costs (Eshed and Lippman 2019). Therefore, it is of great signifcance to study the regulation mechanism of plant height and shoot branching. At present, several dwarf mutants with varying phenotypes have been characterized in cucumber: extremely dwarfed mutant cp, cp-2, scp, csdw, scp-2, cpa, cpa-2, scp-3 (Kubicki et al. 1986; Li et al. 2011a, b; Wang et al. 2017; Hou et al. 2017; Xu et al. 2018; Zhang et al. 2021, 2022, 2024); short internode mutant si, si-2, si107 (Lin et al. 2016; Anarjan et al. 2023; Xu et al. 2023); dwarf mutant dw2 (Li et al. 2022). But most of them own the pleiotropic efects with reduced fertility and weak growth potential which limited the application in variety breeding. The study on lateral branch is relatively independent of plant height, depending on the cucumber varieties which with diferent degrees, CsBRC1 negatively regulates CsPIN3 to inhibit lateral bud growth by afecting the auxin accumulation, and CsBRC1-like might play overlap interaction with CsBRC1 in shoot branching (Shen et al. 2019, 2021). Besides, MADS-box transcription factor CsAGL16 positively regulates axillary bud growth via CsCYP707A4-mediated ABA catabolism in cucumber (Chen et al. 2024). There was no involvement of GAs in lateral branch development has been reported.

In this study, we identifed csdf, a cucumber mutant with an inhibited lateral branch and dwarf phenotype, and isolated CsKAO,the homolog of KAO. A G-to-A mutation happened in the sixth exon alters the mRNA in csdf and prematurely introduces a stop codon, resulting in a deletion of 93 amino acid residues, thereby afecting the catalytic activity of CsKAO and reducing bioactive GAs contents (Fig. 3). The mutations in genes associated with gibberellins biosynthesis always decreases GAs concentrations in plants (Ashikari et al. 2002; Dalmadi et al. 2008; Lin et al. 2023). Besides, the experimental results that dwarf phenotype could be rescued with the exogenous $\mathrm { G A } _ { 3 }$ as well as that the diferential expression of genes related to GA-pathway in csdf confrmed the above speculation (Fig. 6, 7). Overall, it is convincing to conclude that CsaV3_6G006520, a KAO homolog involved in the multi-step catalytic process of ent-kaurenoic acid to $\mathrm { G A } _ { 1 2 }$ , is the candidate gene for the mutant phenotype in csdf.

GAs functioned in diferent processes during the whole life cycle of higher plants, among which plant height was extensively studied by the crucial signaling molecule of GAs (Wu et al. 2023). GAs have been proved to be essential for cell elongation (Gebremeskel et al. 2020), SEM results revealed that the shortened cell length was the reason for the reduced internodes and dwarfsm of csdf (Fig. 1), which might be attributed to the reduced GAs content in csdf. The generation of biologically active GAs relies on the multistep process that catalyzes the formation of individual intermediates (CPS, KS, KO, KAO, GA13ox, GA20ox, GA3ox, GA2ox). Mutations and silencing in those key enzyme genes caused the GA-responsive dwarfsm (Liu et al. 2018; Chen et al. 2019; Wang and Wang 2022). $\mathrm { G A } _ { 1 2 }$ is the intermediate precursor of bioactive gibberellins including $\mathrm { G A } _ { 3 }$ , $\mathrm { G A } _ { 4 }$ and $\mathrm { G A } _ { 7 }$ in vascular plants, KAO functions in the oxidative conversion of the ent-kaurenoic acid to $\mathrm { G A } _ { 1 2 }$ in the endoplasmic reticulum in most plants, while in pea, PsKAO genes were summarized function in the frst two steps but not the fnal transfer step of GA12-aldehyde to $\mathrm { G A } _ { 1 2 }$ (Helliwell et al. 2001; Davidson et al. 2003; Hedden and Thomas 2012). Whether the cucumber KAO homolog shared the conserved function in cucumber has remained unclear. Our results herein indicated that $\mathrm { G A } _ { 1 2 }$ content in csdf is signifcantly decreased compared with that of WT (Fig. 6a), and multiple sequence alignment indicated that the CsKAO shares a high similarity with other KAO homologs (Fig. 4, S5), which points out that CsKAO might be involved in the fnal step of $\mathrm { G A } _ { 1 2 }$ biosynthesis. And the bioactive $\mathrm { G A } _ { 3 }$ and $\mathrm { G A } _ { 4 }$ were decreased signifcantly in csdf (Fig. 6a), which proposed that the abnormal synthesis of $\mathrm { G A } _ { 1 2 }$ ultimately afected the production of bioactive gibberellins. Transcriptome analysis revealed that several genes annotated to GA20ox and GA3ox, both of which functioned in the biosynthesis of bioactive GAs, were diferentially up-regulated in csdf, while GA30ox genes that involved in gibberellin degradation were

down-regulated, those might account for the feedback regulation of GAs (Fig. 7). Several mutants with defective KAO gene have been reported displayed dwarf stature by interfering GAs signaling, such as dwarf2 in sunfower, kao1 kao2 double mutant in Arabidopsis (Fambrini et al. 2011; Regnault et al. 2014). Overall, we speculated that CsKAO shares the highly conservative plant height-regulating mechanism in both monocots and dicots. The defection of CsKAO afects GA biosynthesis and ultimately determines the short internode by interfering with internode cell elongation.

Axillary buds originate from axillary meristems, the development of lateral branches is determined by the initiation of axillary meristems and the outgrowth of axillary buds (Barbier et al. 2019). Since excessive branching in commercial cucumber cultivars negatively interferes with reproductive growth by afecting nutrient allocation and light capture, while stronger lateral branches hold a high potential for improving productivity in the pickling industry of cucumber which is grown in open felds (Ross-Ibarra et al. 2007). Thus, understanding the regulatory mechanisms of lateral branch outgrowth means much to breed cucumber varieties adapted to diferent cultivation systems (Liu et al. 2021). Herein, we found that csdf has normal axillary bud diferentiation but for the depressed lateral branches, the supplementation of GAs in csdf can relieve the inhibition of lateral bud elongation, besides the $\mathrm { G A } _ { 3 }$ with exceeding physiological concentration inhibited the growth of lateral branches in WT (Fig. 8), which point out that GA might be involved in the development of lateral branches in cucumber. The outgrowth of axillary buds is co-regulated by various factors including BRs, SLs, CKs, GAs and sugars to control shoot branching (Xia et al. 2021). The disruption of csdf-mediated GA biosynthesis alters gene expression of the other hormone signaling network (Fig. 7). Auxin was considered the key mediator accounting for apical dominance to restrain lateral bud outgrowth (Domagalska and Leyser 2011), the excessive accumulation of auxin in lateral branches inhibit axillary bud outgrowth in cucumber (Shen et al. 2021). Auxin repressed the axillary bud development by inhibiting cytokinin content in stems and CK transportation to axillary bud (Su et al. 2011). The decrease in IAA content in the terminal bud and the increased tendency in the lateral bud in csdf might be the component factor for the weakness of apical dominance. We speculated GA and IAA do have some possibility of interactive regulation in the branching of cucumber. Previous studies revealed that homeostasis and crosstalk of multiple hormones play an important role in controlling plant height, it is worthwhile investigating whether there are cross-talks between phytohormones in lateral branches in cucumber. In addition, several genes in the JA signaling pathway are diferentially expressed in csdf, indicating GA might have an interaction with JA in

regulating plant architecture, but the specifc molecular interaction mechanism requires further research.

In summary, we put forward that the mutation of CsKAO in csdf leads to the phenotype of dwarf plant height and shorter branches, and those efects are caused by CsKAO’s participation in GA biosynthesis. Those fundings are valuable for elucidating the mechanisms of plant height, and point out the potential of GA-mutants for breeders to optimize plant architecture in Cucurbit crops. The complex relationships among diferent hormones in branches of csdf deserve further investigation.
