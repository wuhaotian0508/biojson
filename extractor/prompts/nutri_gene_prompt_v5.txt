#### **`<Instruct>`**

**Step 1: Literature Analysis & Core Gene Identification**

Use the **thinking** tool to think about the following questions

1.  **In-depth Reading**: Read the full text of the literature uploaded by the user.
2. **Step-by-step Thinking**:
   - Is this paper a research paper or a review paper, and what is its core scientific question regarding nutritional metabolites?
   - **[Critical Step] Identify Core Genes**:
    (a) for research paper: Which gene or genes influnceing the nutritional metabolite(s) are the primary subjects of experimentation in the **Results** section?  Clearly distinguish these **Core Genes** from **background genes** that are only mentioned in the Introduction or Discussion for contextual purposes.
    (b) forreview paper: Which gene or genes influnceing the nutritional metabolite(s) are the primary subjects of summarization in the paper?
   - For each reported gene–metabolite relationship, clearly identify the gene's source species (the species the gene comes from) and the applied species (the species in which the metabolic product or effect is investigated), and do not merge them.
   - These Core Genes are specifically classified as either "Pathway Gene" (primarily enzyme genes acting as catalysts) or "Regulatory Gene" (primarily transcription factor genes acting as regulators); all other Core Genes (such as genes with fuction of transporter, storage, sequestration, etc.) are categorized as "Common Gene".

**Step 2: Structured Information Extraction**

Based on the analysis results obtained from "Step 1" and the identified "Core Gene list", please call the **structured_information** tool to extract the structure information.

**Core Principles**:
- **Focus on Core Genes**: Only extract the relevant information of the genes that were determined as "Core Genes" in Step 1.
- **Follow the Original Text**: Only extract the information that is explicitly stated or can be strongly inferred from the charts and tables in the literature.
- **Add Standard Identifiers for Supplementary Information**: For information such as species and functional classification, if the original text does not provide it but can be clearly inferred, then a standard identifier should be added.
- **Leave Blank Fields**: If specific fields of the literature are not provided, fill with 'NA'.

**Step 3: Verification**

In order to ensure the accuracy of the extracted structured information, you need to use the **thinking** tool to think carefully and verify the structured information again.

If there are any errors, you can call the **structured_information** tool to make corrections again.
